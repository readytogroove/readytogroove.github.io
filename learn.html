Learn about lady parts

  • Fallopian Tubes
  • Ovaries
  • Uterus
  • Cervix
  • Vagina

Fallopian Tubes – These arm-like structures guide the recently ovulated egg (or ovum) towards the uterus (or womb). If fertilization of the ovum occurs, it will take place inside the fallopian tubes. The fertilized egg (or embryo), is then guided to the uterus for implantation.

Ovaries – The ovaries are small, almond-sized structures that each house around half a million ovarian follicles.

Uterus – The uterus (or womb) houses a developing fetus during pregnancy. Each cycle, in response to hormone surges, the lining of the uterus thickens to prepare for the possible implantation of a fertilized egg (or embryo).

Cervix – The cervix is the pathway between the uterus and the vagina. As ovulation approaches mid-cycle, the cervix produces a special fluid that helps with sperm survival and motility in an otherwise hostile vaginal environment.

Vagina – The vagina (or birth canal) is the pathway between the cervix and the external female genitalia (or vulva). It maintains a very acidic environment to protect against infection, and this environment is hostile to sperm without the presence of cervical fluid.

Why is this info so important?

Take control of your reproductive health

Groove helps you understand how your body changes throughout your cycle, which can help you detect when something goes awry. Understanding the female reproductive system can provide you with important information to bring to your doctor. This can help in both the diagnosis and treatment of many reproductive disorders.

Know when you're fertile

Familiarizing yourself with your body can help you determine exactly when you're fertile. This helps you achieve your goals, whether you're looking to naturally avoid pregnancy or you are interested in getting pregnant.

Know when to expect your period

Learning about your lady parts can help you predict the first day of your next period with incredible accuracy—down to the day! This is useful for many reasons, but, best of all, it gives you amazing insight into the functioning of your body's internal clock.
